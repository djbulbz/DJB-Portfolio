About

Hi! I’m glad that you want to get to know me better! And even if you didn’t, welcome!

My name is Deborah (you can call me whatever you’d like as far as nicknames go, I’ve heard them all).

Born and raised in New Jersey, I still live in West Orange where innovation has lived, thrived and evolved.

Now keep reading below for the mandatory bio in the third person.

Deborah graduated cum laude from Caldwell University with a BA in biology and minors in English and chemistry. During her time in college, she was able to explore different interests including conducting research in both public health and microbiology and willingly adding to her workload (by almost double-majoring in English) while listening to her peers complain about theirs.

She previously was a box-checker, checking off items on the never-ending list of accomplishments needed to succeed in life, until she read The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks. It was then that she realized that she needed to combine her love for science and writing in order to communicate the stories that no one ever hears about. (Also her mother always told her that she was going to be a writer someday since she was young, but she never believed her.)

Currently, she works as a freelance reporter for TAPinto West Orange, a local online newspaper, constantly honing her skills to be as good of a communicator as she can be (she’s gotten a lot of positive feedback so far).

When she’s not rolling her eyes at how pretentious writing in the third person is, she also enjoys watching slice-of-life anime, catching up on kdramas, listening to J-Rock and K-Pop, reading, and playing visual novels and other video games.

If you’re based in New Jersey (or even if you’re not) please feel free to reach out. I’m only an email away at djbalthazar15@gmail.com
